Rechargeable Batteries

Rechargeable batteries are a great way to save money and reduce waste. They can be recharged hundreds of times and are available in a variety of sizes and chemistries. Some common types of rechargeable batteries include:

  • Nickel Cadmium (Ni-Cd)
  • Nickel Metal Hydride (Ni-MH)
  • Lithium Ion (Li-Ion)
  • Lithium Polymer (Li-Po)

Tips for Using Rechargeable Batteries

  • Charge batteries fully before use
  • Use a charger designed for the type of battery you have
  • Do not mix batteries of different types or brands
  • Store batteries in a cool, dry place

Single Use Batteries

Single use batteries, also known as disposable batteries, are designed to be used once and then discarded. They are available in a variety of sizes and chemistries, including:

  • Alkaline
  • Lithium
  • Zinc Carbon
  • Zinc Chloride

Tips for Using Single Use Batteries

  • Remove batteries from devices that will not be used for a while
  • Do not mix batteries of different types or brands
  • Store batteries in a cool, dry place
  • Dispose of batteries properly
